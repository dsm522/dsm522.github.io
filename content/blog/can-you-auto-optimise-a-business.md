Can you auto-optimise a business?

What caught my eye in Andrej Karpathy's recent AI experiment was this simple loop:

- An agent makes a change.
- It gets tested.
- The result is scored.
- The next version learns from that and tries again.

Run that often enough and the gains compound.

What makes this interesting is the question it forces you to ask:

Where in a business do you have a workflow that can actually be scored well enough to improve automatically?

That is the real constraint.

*You cannot auto-optimise what you cannot score.*

And once you can score it, the governance questions come next:

- Who decides what "better" means?
- What stops the agent gaming the metric?
- What gets versioned, audited and rolled back?
- Who owns experiment 34 at 2am if the overnight loop breaks something important?

It feels like there is a real opportunity here for companies that can define a tight workflow, build a credible eval around it, and improve it safely inside clear boundaries.

While most businesses are still thinking in terms of pilots, the bigger shift may be building optimisation loops.

That is where this starts getting interesting.
